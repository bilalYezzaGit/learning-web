:::question
$\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^5 - 1}{x - 1} = $
:::

:::option
$1$
:::

:::option{correct}
$5$
:::

:::option
$4$
:::

:::option
$0$
:::

:::explanation
On reconnait le taux de variation de $g(x) = x^5$ en $a = 1$ :

$$\frac{x^5 - 1}{x - 1} = \frac{g(x) - g(1)}{x - 1}$$

On calcule $g'(x) = 5x^4$, d'ou $g'(1) = 5 \times 1^4 = 5$.

Par suite, $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^5 - 1}{x - 1} = g'(1) = 5$.
:::
